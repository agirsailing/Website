Milestones timeline

Whats UP? March

2026


This month has been all about turning ideas into something more tangible as our SuMoth project at KTH in Stockholm continues to take shape.


On the technical side, one of the biggest milestones has been modifying our hull. The rear end of the kayak has now been removed, marking the first real structural step in transforming it into our future foiling boat. At the same time, the team has been working intensively on the digital design, and a full CAD model of the boat has now been created. We’re currently putting the finishing touches on the design to finalize the last details before moving further into the build process.

Beyond the workshop and the computer screens, we also visited the boat convention “Allt för Sjön”, where we spent time looking for potential sponsors and partners while also gathering inspiration from the broader sailing and marine community.

Internally, we’ve continued strengthening the team with several larger meetings involving everyone in the project, making sure we stay aligned as the work progresses. We’ve also taken time to build the team outside of work, including a movie night that gave us the chance to relax together and get to know each other even better.

And lastly, we’ve been working on something a little different behind the scenes, our first vlog! It has now been filmed and edited, and we’re excited to share it with you very soon. Step by step, the project is moving forward, and we’re getting closer to seeing the boat come to life. Stay tuned for more updates as we continue the journey toward Garda this summer.

Whats UP? February

2026


This month has been a really exciting step forward for our SuMoth journey here at KTH in Stockholm, with several major developments bringing the project closer to reality.


We’re happy to share that we’ve started building strong connections outside the team as well, and have already found new sponsors and partners who believe in what we’re working towards. Their support is an important boost as we continue shaping the foundation of our boat and our campaign toward Garda.

On the technical side, we’ve made great progress with some key resources falling into place. We’ve received a kayak that will serve as the hull for our boat, giving us a concrete starting point for the build.

In addition, we’ve been given the opportunity to borrow a Mach2 moth, which has been incredibly valuable for taking measurements and gathering inspiration.

At the same time, we’ve been diving deeper into the engineering work behind the scenes. This month included material testing as well as force and load analyses, helping us better understand the demands on the structure and guiding the design choices we’ll make going forward. Everything is starting to come together, and the momentum is building fast.

We’re looking forward to sharing even more progress next month as we keep pushing toward our goal of competing in Garda this summer.

Whats UP? January

2026


We are at the very beginning of our journey as a brand new SuMoth team from KTH Royal Institute of Technology in Stockholm. We’re currently in the final stages of structuring the team, defining roles, and laying the foundation for the work ahead.



Over the past weeks, we’ve held two larger meetings with the entire team, giving us the chance to align our goals, get to know each other better, and build a shared vision for the project. Alongside this, the board has had several internal meetings, focusing on organization, planning, and making sure we’re setting ourselves up in the best possible way moving forward.

We’ve also been talking to people from other teams to learn from their experiences. One highlight was a larger meeting with Chalmers Formula Sailing Team, who shared valuable insights, tips, and advice that have already helped guide our next steps.

Our goal is clear: to be on the water and compete in Garda this summer. There’s a lot of work ahead, but these first few weeks have been all about building strong foundations, and we’re excited to keep you updated as the journey continues.

Whats UP? December

2025


After deciding to participate in the 2026 edition, our team was officialy created with high ambitions and determination.


We decided to do everything in our power in order to sail a moth in the 2026 edition of the SuMoth challenge. The countdown has already started and we have five months to design, build and test our moth. The next months will be full of work from sponsor scouting,to material and component purchasingand testing, to design and looking for innovative solutions that can be tested and implemented as fast as possible.
